## 1. Book on the right day

Contrary to the "Tuesday myth", what matters is the **day you travel**, not the
day you book. For London–Bangkok, Thursday departures are typically 8–12%
cheaper than Friday/Saturday ones.

## 2. Shoulder-season is king

Late April–early June and October–November give you dry weather in Thailand
and the cheapest fares of the year — often £450 return vs £750 in December.

## 3. Mix carriers with self-transfer

Kiwi.com's virtual interlining pairs low-costs like Wizz Air (to Abu Dhabi)
with Etihad (to BKK) for savings of £100–£200. Bring carry-on only.

## 4. Use flexible-date matrices

Google Flights' "Whole month" view and Skyscanner's "Cheapest month" surface
fares that calendar search hides.

## 5. Hunt error fares

Follow @SecretFlying and HolidayPirates — 2–3 London↔BKK error fares slip
through every year, some under £300 return.

## 6. Fly into secondary airports

Don Mueang (DMK) is often £40 cheaper than Suvarnabhumi (BKK). A 30-min
taxi connects them.

## 7. Use a travel rewards card

Barclaycard Avios Plus earns ~2.5% in Avios — enough for a free short-haul
every 3–4 long-haul trips.
